Our Programs

Expanding opportunity through academic support, mentorship, and community-driven learning.

Academic Support Program

Our Academic Support Program provides structured tutoring, homework assistance, and exam preparation for internally displaced students.

  • Subject-specific tutoring
  • Study groups and collaborative sessions
  • Exam preparation workshops
  • Literacy and numeracy support

Peer Mentorship Program

Our Peer Mentorship Program pairs upperclass students with younger learners to provide guidance, accountability, and emotional support.

  • One-on-one mentorship
  • Leadership development for mentors
  • Weekly check-ins
  • Goal-setting and progress tracking

Learning Hub Initiative

Learning Hubs provide safe, resource-rich spaces where displaced students can study, access materials, and participate in structured learning activities.

  • Textbooks and essential school supplies
  • Quiet study areas
  • Access to trained peer mentors
  • Workshops and enrichment sessions

Community Engagement & Outreach

This program strengthens connections between schools, families, and communities to support displaced learners.

  • Parent engagement sessions
  • School-community partnerships
  • Resource drives for books and supplies
  • Awareness and advocacy campaigns

Leadership & Skills Development

We equip students with essential life and leadership skills that extend beyond the classroom.

  • Communication and teamwork
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Public speaking workshops
  • Career and academic planning
