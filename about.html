Who We Are

Carol’s Prep Academy is redefining what equitable education looks like for displaced and underserved students in Cameroon.

As a community-rooted learning initiative, we are one of the first programs in the region to build a peer-led academic model that empowers students from Form 1 through Upper Sixth. Through collaborative learning circles, structured mentorship, and resource-equipped community hubs, our students develop the confidence, academic readiness, and leadership skills needed to thrive in secondary school, university, and beyond.

Why We Exist

More than 200,000 young people in Cameroon have been displaced by ongoing conflict, leaving students without stable classrooms, learning materials, or academic support. Many high‑achieving students fall behind not because of ability, but because they lack the resources, mentorship, and community networks needed to thrive.

Carol’s Prep Academy exists to close this gap. We create learning environments where displaced students feel supported, challenged, and connected, restoring hope and rebuilding pathways to opportunity.

Our Mission

To build inclusive learning communities that empower displaced and underserved students through peer collaboration, mentorship, and access to essential educational resources.

Our Vision

A Cameroon where every young person regardless of their income or background has the support, confidence, and opportunity to succeed in school and lead in their community.

Our Founding Story

Carol’s Prep Academy began in 2020 when we saw brilliant, motivated students struggling not because of a lack of potential, but because conflict had disrupted their education. Many were attending overcrowded classrooms, studying without books, and navigating academic challenges alone.

We started with a simple idea: students learn best when they learn together. What began as small peer‑study circles quickly grew into a structured model of mentorship, leadership development, and community‑based learning hubs. Today, Carol’s Prep Academy continues to expand, driven by the belief that every student deserves a chance to thrive.

Our Approach

Peer Learning

Students collaborate in structured study circles that strengthen understanding and build confidence.

Mentorship

Upperclassmen are trained to guide younger peers, model leadership, and support academic growth.

Learning Hubs

Safe, resource‑equipped spaces where displaced students can study, access materials, and receive support.

Community Partnerships

We work with schools, families, and local leaders to build sustainable, community‑rooted learning systems.

Our Impact

Carol’s Prep Academy is transforming learning for displaced students across Cameroon by strengthening academic performance, rebuilding confidence, and creating peer‑led communities where young people lift each other up. Our model is proving that when students are supported, connected, and equipped, they thrive even in the most challenging circumstances.

Academic Growth

Students participating in our peer‑learning circles show stronger comprehension, improved study habits, and higher exam performance. Our structured mentorship model ensures that no student studies alone and every learner has someone guiding, supporting, and encouraging them.

Leadership Development

Peer mentors gain confidence, communication skills, and a sense of responsibility as they support younger students. Many go on to lead clubs, volunteer groups, and community initiatives, becoming role models within their schools.

Community Resilience

Our learning hubs provide safe, resource‑equipped spaces where displaced youth can study, connect, and rebuild a sense of belonging. These hubs strengthen community ties and offer stability in regions affected by conflict.
